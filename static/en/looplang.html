ALL (A Loopy Language) is a constructed language with a very particular visual style.

Alphabet

ALL's alphabet consists of only 6 letters and 4 connectors, though each letter/connector does not necessarily map to a sound, often it takes multiple letters to represent a sound.

ALL makes a distinction between 'up' loops and 'down' loops. A loop is 'up' if you start and end it at the bottom, it is 'down' if you start and end it at the top.

ALL loops can also be doubled by adding a little circle at the far end of the loop. They can be tripled by adding two little loops at the end.

Vowels are made via connectors that switch the direction of the loops.

Words

ALL is highly synthetic, and each word comprises its own entire sentence!

Within a 'word' nouns will always begin and end with consonants, while verbs and verbal elements will always begin and end with vowels. Additionally, no verb will begin with a nominal ending.
