Sentence Structure

Word Order

Shmigan mandates a Object-Subject-Verb word order. Adjectives appear before the nouns they modify, while adverbs appear after the verbs they modify. Words are identified by ending with a non-empty coda. As such, shmigan does not use spaces. Nouns are identified by beginning with an article, which no base words begin with. Verbs are identified by uniquely following the subject in the sentence. Additionally verbs have conjugatory prefices.

Subordinate Clauses

Subordinate clauses are inserted as if they were just a very long adjective or adverb.

Plurality and Possessivity

Shmigan has no plurality or possessivity.

Punctuation

Punctuation is indicated by adding a slash inside the tail of a symbol. There are, in total, 9 different punctuation marks. They are:

Mark Meaning
< Begin quotative phrase. Similar to left " in English.
> End quotative phrase. Similar to right " in English.
. End statement. Similar to . in English.
[ Begin ironic/sarcastic phrase. Similar to beginning italics in English.
] End ironic/sarcastic phrase. Similar to ending italics in English.
? End question statement. Similar to ? in English.
( Begin parenthetical statement. Similar to ( in English.
) End parenthetical statement. Similar to ) in English.
! End exclamatory statement. Similar to ! in English.

Conjugation

Verbs in Shmigan have tense, aspect, voice, and mood. They do not have number or persons, which are identified by the nouns. The conjugation of the verb is indicated by a single symbol appended to the beginning of the verb. Note on dynamic vs static passive voices. A passive voice is static if the subject is acted upon at a certain time, dynamic if the subject got itself acted upon. Static passives tend to use the word 'be' in english, while dynamic passives tend to use the word 'get'. Note that participles and infinitives take the same form, the 's' prefix. And still have aspects (though tend to use the habitual).

Tense

Shmigan has 3 tenses: Past, Present, and Future. The present is indicated by a simple prefix. The past is indicated by an s-addition prefix. The future is indicated by a compound prefix.

Aspect

Shmigan has 5 aspects: Simple, Perfect, Progressive, Perfect Progressive, and Habitual. The Simple aspect is indicated by the /a/ vowel. The Perfect aspect is indicated by the /e/ vowel. The Progressive aspect is indicated by the /u/ vowel. The Perfect Progressive aspect is indicated by the /o/ vowel. The Habitual aspect is indicated by the /i/ vowel.

Voice

Shmigan has 3 voices: Active, Static Passive, and Dynamic Passive. The active voice is indicated by the lack of a symbol on the tail. The static passive voice is indicated by a dashed tail. The dynamic passive voice is indicated by a slashed tail.

Mood

Shmigan has 3 moods: Indicative, Imperative, and Interrogative. The indicative mood is indicated by a tail on the right. The imperative mood is indicated by a tail on the left. The interrogative mood is indicated by a tail on both sides.

Derivational Morphology

Derivational morphology in Shmigan is achieved via word suffixes. Participlization and verbization are achieved by merely using the word where a different part of speech would normally occur.
