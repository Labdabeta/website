Shmigan Orthography

Vowels

Vowels are signified by the content in what is called the 'bucket' of the letter. There are only five vowels and diphthongization is very rare. The vowels are:

Symbol Sound
' /i/
" /e/
- /a/
-' /u/
-" /o/

Syllables

Shmigan uses a syllabary, which means that each symbol represents an entire syllable (for the most part). Shmigan recognizes 4 distinct syllable types. They are:

Symbol Type
A Stop/Nasal
E Fricative/Approximant
O Compound

Conjugation

Verbs in shmigan have tense, aspect, voice, and mood. They do not have numbers or persons, which are identified by the nouns. The conjugation of a verb is indicated by a single symbol appended to the beginning of the verb. Note on dynamic vs static passive voices. A passive voice is static if the subject is acted upon at a certain time, dynamic if the subject got itself acted upon. Static passives tend to use the word 'be' in english, while dynamic passives tend to use the word 'get'.

Tense

Shmigan has 3 tenses: Past, Present, and Future. The past is indicated by a stop/nasal prefix. The present is indicated by a fricative/approximant prefix. The future is indicated by a compound prefix.

Aspect

Shmigan has 5 aspects: Simple, Perfect, Progressive, Perfect Progressive, and Habitual. The simple aspect is indicated by the /a/ vowel. The Perfect aspect is indicated by the /e/ vowel. The progressive aspect is indicated by the /u/ vowel. The perfect progressive aspect is indicated by the /o/ vowel. The Habitual aspect is indicated by the /i/ vowel.

Voice

Shmigan has 3 voices: Active, Static Passive, and Dynamic Passive. The active voice is indicated by the lack of a symbol on the tail. The static passive voice is indicated by a doubled tail. The dynamic passive voice is indicated by a slashed tail.

Mood

Shmigan has 3 moods: Indicative, Conditional, and Interrogative. The indicative is indicated by a tail on the right. The conditional is indicated by a tail on the left. The interrogative is indicated by both tails. If neither tail is present, it indicates the imperative mood, which is always active.

Combining all of these leads to a total of 3*5*3*3 + 3*5 = 150 conjugations. You can view all of them in Shmigan endings and in English conjugation here.

Punctuation

Punctuation is indicated by adding a circle to the tail of a symbol. There are, in total, 9 different punctuation marks. They are:

Mark Meaning
:.A Begin quotative phrase. Similar to left " in English.
;,A End quotative phrase. Similar to right " in English.
:;.,A End statement. Similar to . in English.
:.E Begin ironic/sarcastic phrase. Similar to beginning italics in English.
;,E End ironic/sarcastic phrase. Similar to ending italics in English.
:;.,E End question statement. Similar to ? in English.
:.O Begin parenthetical statement. Similar to ( in English.
;,O End parenthetical statement. Similar to ) in English.
:;.,O End exclamatory statement. Similar to ! in English.
