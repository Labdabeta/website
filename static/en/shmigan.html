Shmigan Phonology

Shmigan has no nasals and does not differentiate between voicings. It has a lot of different fricatives. There are no labial sounds whatsoever in shmigan (thouh some speakers occasionally labialize /t/, /n/, and /θ/ to /p/, /m/, and /f/ in certain situations).

Shmigan contains only 5 vowels: close front unrounded, close back rounded, open-mid front unrounded, close-mid back rounded, and open front unrounded. There are no dipthongs in shmigan (though some speakers replace vowel+h+vowel with a dipthong.

As an onset, any consonant may appear. Additionally, the voiceless alveolar fricative may appear followed by a voiceless plosive, a nasal, the voiceles dental fricative, the voiceless post alveolar fricative, or an approximant. On top of that, a voiceless alveolar fricative followed by a voiceless plosive may also be followed by the alveolar approximant.

Codas can also contain any consonant. Additionally, all double-consonant onsets may appear reversed in the coda. Finally, a voiced alveolar plosive followed by a voiceless alveolar plosive, an alveolar fricative followed by a postalveolar fricative, or an alveolar approximant followed by a lateral approximant may appear.

Shmigan Alphabet

Shmigan uses a compound alphabet, with the onset of a syllable attached directly to its vowel. Only coda's appear without a vowel. The vowels are: i /i/, e /e/, a /a/, u /u/, and o /o/. They appear in what is called the 'bucket' of the letter.

The 'head' of the letter indicates what type of consonant cluster it represents. ^ represents a simple cluster, ~ represents an s-addition cluster, and * represents a compound cluster.

Conjugation

Verbs in Shmigan have tense, aspect, voice, and mood. They do not have number or persons, which are identified by the nouns. The conjugation of the verb is indicated by a single symbol appended to the beginning of the verb. Note on dynamic vs static passive voices. A passive voice is static if the subject is acted upon at a certain time, dynamic if the subject got itself acted upon. Static passives tend to use the word 'be' in english, while dynamic passives tend to use the word 'get'.

Tense

Shmigan has 3 tenses: Past, Present, and Future. The present is indicated by a simple prefix. The past is indicated by an s-addition prefix. The future is indicated by a compound prefix.

Aspect

Shmigan has 5 aspects: Simple, Perfect, Progressive, Perfect Progressive, and Habitual. The Simple aspect is indicated by the /a/ vowel. The Perfect aspect is indicated by the /e/ vowel. The Progressive aspect is indicated by the /u/ vowel. The Perfect Progressive aspect is indicated by the /o/ vowel. The Habitual aspect is indicated by the /i/ vowel.

Voice

Shmigan has 3 voices: Active, Static Passive, and Dynamic Passive. The active voice is indicated by the lack of a symbol on the tail. The static passive voice is indicated by a doubled tail. The dynamic passive voice is indicated by a slashed tail.

Mood

Shmigan has 3 moods: Indicative, Imperative, and Interrogative. The indicative mood is indicated by a tail on the right. The imperative mood is indicated by a tail on the left. The interrogative mood is indicated by a tail on both sides.

Sentence Structure

Word Order

Shmigan mandates an Object-Subject-Verb word order. Adjectives appear before the nouns they modify, while adverbs appear after the verbs they modify. Note that words can be identified by ending with a non-empty coda. As such, Shmigan does not use spaces.

Subordinate Clauses

Subordinate clauses are inserted as if they were a single noun. They can be identified when a sentence appears to have too many nouns (without listing conjunctions).

Punctuation

Shmigan does not use spaces. Instead, words can be identified easily as all words end in a consonant, which will have nothing in its bucket.

Punctuation is indicated by adding a slash inside the tail of a symbol. There are, in total, 9 different punctuation marks. They are:

Mark Meaning
< Begin quotative phrase. Similar to left " in English.
> End quotative phrase. Similar to right " in English.
. End statement. Similar to . in English.
[ Begin ironic/sarcastic phrase. Similar to beginning italics in English.
] End ironic/sarcastic phrase. Similar to ending italics in English.
? End question statement. Similar to ? in English.
( Begin parenthetical statement. Similar to ( in English.
) End parenthetical statement. Similar to ) in English.
! End exclamatory statement. Similar to ! in English.
